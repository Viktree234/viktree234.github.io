Physics 104 – Quick Formula Sheet

Electrostatics, Capacitance & Dielectrics

Coulomb's law: \( F = \frac{1}{4\pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)
Electric field: \( E = \frac{1}{4\pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{Q}{r^2} \)
Potential: \( V = \frac{1}{4\pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{Q}{r} \)
Capacitance: \( C = \frac{Q}{V} \)
Parallel plate: \( C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d} \)
With dielectric: \( C = \kappa \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d} \)
Energy stored: \( U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2 = \frac{Q^2}{2C} = \frac{1}{2} QV \)

Current Electricity

Ohm's law: \( V = IR \)
Current: \( I = \frac{Q}{t} \)
Resistance: \( R = \rho \frac{L}{A} \)
Power: \( P = VI = I^2R = \frac{V^2}{R} \)
Drift velocity: \( v_d = \frac{I}{nAe} \)

Magnetic Fields

Magnetic flux: \( \Phi_B = BA \cos\theta \)
Biot–Savart: \( dB = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{Idl \sin\theta}{r^2} \)
Ampere's law: \( \oint B \, dl = \mu_0 I \)

Forces on Charge & Conductor

Lorentz force: \( F = q(E + vB \sin\theta) \)
Conductor: \( F = BIL \sin\theta \)
Radius: \( r = \frac{mv}{qB} \)
Time period: \( T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB} \)

Electromagnetic Induction

Faraday: \( \varepsilon = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \)
Straight conductor: \( \varepsilon = BLv \)
Self-inductance: \( \varepsilon = -L \frac{dI}{dt} \)
Energy in inductor: \( U = \frac{1}{2} L I^2 \)

AC Circuits

Instantaneous emf: \( E = E_0 \sin \omega t \)
RMS: \( V_{rms} = \frac{V_0}{\sqrt 2}, \ I_{rms} = \frac{I_0}{\sqrt 2} \)
Reactances: \( X_L = \omega L, \ X_C = \frac{1}{\omega C} \)
Impedance: \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \)
Phase angle: \( \tan \phi = \frac{X_L - X_C}{R} \)
Power: \( P = VI \cos\phi \)

Semiconductors

Resistivity: \( \rho = \frac{1}{\sigma} = \frac{1}{n e \mu} \)
Current density: \( J = \sigma E = nq\mu E \)
Diode current: \( I = I_0 (e^{qV/kT} - 1) \)
